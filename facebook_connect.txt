  Instagram Configuration Steps
  To use Instagram automation, you must have an Instagram Business or Creator account connected to a Facebook Page. Personal accounts won't work with the Graph API.
  Step 1: Create a Facebook App
  1. Go to Meta for Developers
  2. Click My Apps → Create App
  3. Select "Other" → "Business" app type
  4. Give it a name (e.g., "ParroByte CRM")
  Step 2: Add Instagram Products
  1. In your app's dashboard, click Add Product
  2. Add Instagram Basic Display (for authentication)
  3. Add Instagram Graph API (for reading comments and replying)
  Step 3: Get App Credentials
  1. Go to Settings → Basic
  2. Copy App ID and App Secret
  3. Add them to your .env file:
     FACEBOOK_APP_ID=your_app_id
     FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET=your_app_secret
     INSTAGRAM_REDIRECT_URI=http://localhost:3000/instagram/oauth/callback
  Step 4: Configure OAuth Redirect URI
  1. Go to Products → Facebook Login → Settings (or Instagram Basic Display → Basic Display)
  2. Add your redirect URI exactly as:
     http://localhost:3000/instagram/oauth/callback
  3. Save changes
  Step 5: Convert Instagram to Business/Creator Account
  1. Open Instagram app → Go to your profile
  2. Menu → Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account
  3. Choose Business or Creator
  4. Connect it to a Facebook Page (create one if needed)
  Step 6: Add Testers (if app is in Development mode)
  1. In your Facebook App, go to Roles → Testers
  2. Add your Instagram account's Facebook profile as a Tester
  3. Accept the invitation from your Facebook notifications
  Step 7: Required Permissions
  The app requests these scopes automatically:
  • instagram_basic — read your media
  • instagram_manage_comments — read and reply to comments
  • pages_read_engagement — access pages connected to your IG account
  Step 8: Connect in the CRM
  1. Restart your server (npm run dev)
  2. Go to Instagram Automation page
  3. Click Connect Instagram
  4. Log in with the Facebook account that owns the Page connected to your Instagram Business account
  5. Grant all permissions

  Quick Debugging Checklist for YouTube
  If comments with "website" still don't trigger replies:
  1. Click "Test Your Rules" on the YouTube page, type website, and verify your rule shows MATCH
  2. Click "Debug Comments", enter a video ID, and check the "clean text" column — it should show website without HTML tags
  3. Check the "Last Run Log" after clicking "Process Now" — look for:
    • info — how many comments were found
    • skip — reasons for skipping (own comment, already replied, no rule matched)
    • match / success — when a reply was sent
  4. Make sure your rule is Active (green badge), not Paused
